How to choose a computer, laptop or phone?
Which specifications to look out for?

Last Update: 7/10/2024

Raquel Violeta (Rachel Violet)

Choosing a new computer, laptop or phone, can be quite difficult for the average user. With this in mind, I've decided to write this article to help you choose your new device. Please, keep in mind that this article reflects the author's personal preference.

(A)ndroid phone or tablet (i)OS phone or tablet, (iPhone or iPad) (W)indows computer or laptop (L)inux computer or laptop (M)acOS computer or laptop (iMac or MacBook) (F)inal considerations

Android phone

Operating System

Android is the most popular mobile operating system. It is known for being highly customizable.

Minimum: Android 12

Recommended: Android 14 or later

Safest choice: Android 15 or later

It is not safe to use older versions of Android.

RAM memory

RAM memory is where apps temporarily place their data. The greater the amount of RAM, the better.

Minimum: 4 GB RAM

Recommended: 6 GB RAM or higher

Safest choice: 8 GB RAM or higher

Processor or CPU (Central Processing Unit)

Think of it as the device's brain.

Minimum: 2.0 GHz ~ 2.5 GHz

Recommended: 2.5 GHz or higher

Safest choice: 3.0 GHz or higher

Graphics Card or GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

The GPU is very useful for 3D rendering. Powerful GPUs improve your experience with games and video editing.

For most phones, the GPU comes integrated with the CPU. A good CPU/Chipset will give you a good GPU.

Display or screen

Choose a screen you're comfortable with.

Minimum: HD+ (900p) + 60hz

Recommended: Full HD (1080p) + 60hz

Safest choice: Full HD (1080p) or higher + 60hz or higher

Most phones have a Full HD resolution. The greater the resolution and refresh rate, the more battery the screen will consume.

Storage

The storage is where your documents and files are stored.

Minimum: 64 GB

Recommended: 128 GB or higher

Safest choice: 128 GB or higher

Camera

The hardware used to take pictures and record videos.

Minimum: 10 MP, 1080p @ 30FPS

Recommended: 12 MP, 1080p @ 30FPS or greater

Safest choice: +12MP, 4K @ +30FPS or greater

It is recommended to choose a camera that can record 1080p videos. They are likely to also take Full HD pictures.

Battery

The battery powers the device.

Minimum: 2000 mAh

Recommended: 3000 mAh or greater

Safest choice: 5000 mAh or greater

Additional factors, like your usage pattern and your screen brightness, are just as important for battery life.

Extras

There is nothing wrong about choosing devices with extra features, like fast charging.

iOS (iPhone or iPad)

iPhones:

Minimum: iPhone Xr, Xs and SE 2nd generation series or newer

Safest choice: The latest iPhone available: iPhone 15 series as of July 2024

iPads:

Minimum: iPad Mini 5th, iPad 6th, iPad Air 3rd, iPad Pro 2nd or newer

Safest choice: The latest iPad available.

Windows PC/Laptop

Operating System

Microsoft Windows is the most used operating system for desktops and laptops. It is known for its support for popular applications.

Minimum: Windows 10

Recommended: Windows 10 or newer

Safest choice: Windows 11 or newer

It is not safe to use older versions of Windows.

RAM memory

RAM memory is where apps temporarily place their data. The greater the amount of RAM, the better.

Minimum: 4 GB RAM

Recommended: 8 GB RAM or higher

Safest choice: 16 GB RAM or higher

Processor or CPU (Central Processing Unit)

Think of it as the device's brain.

Minimum: 2.5 GHz

Recommended: 2.5 GHz or higher

Safest choice: 3.0 GHz or higher

Graphics Card or GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

The GPU is very useful for 3D rendering. Video games and video editing is made easier with this hardware.

Minimum: Any GPU will do for most tasks.

Recommended: RTX 2000s series, RX 6000s series or greater

Safest choice: Latest GPU available

Display or screen

The display is what you can see.

Minimum: HD+ (900p) + 60hz

Recommended: Full HD (1080p) + 60hz

Safest choice: 4K or higher + 60hz or higher

For laptops, the greater the resolution and refresh rate, the more battery the screen will consume.

Storage

The storage is where your documents and files are stored.

Minimum: 256 GB

Recommended: 512 GB or higher

Safest choice: 1024 GB (1 TB) or higher

Camera

The hardware used to take pictures and record videos.

Minimum: 10 MP, 1080p @ 30FPS

Recommended: 12 MP, 1080p @ 30FPS or greater

Safest choice: +12MP, 4K @ +30FPS or greater

It is recommended to choose a camera that can record 1080p videos. They are likely to also take Full HD pictures.

Battery

The battery powers the device.

Recommended: 30Wh or greater

Additional factors, like your usage pattern and your screen brightness, are just as important for battery life.

Power supply

The power supply powers desktop computers.

The recommended power supply depends on your computer's specs and usage. If you are buying a pre-built computer, it may not be necessary to switch it.

Linux PC/Laptop

Operating System

The Linux operating system (known as GNU/Unix) is the most customizable desktop and laptop operating system.

Recommended for beginners: Debian, Ubuntu or Linux Mint

Keep in mind that Debian, Ubuntu and Linux Mint aren't operating systems, they are just Linux distributions.

RAM memory

RAM memory is where apps temporarily place their data. The greater the amount of RAM, the better.

Minimum: 4 GB RAM

Recommended: 6 GB RAM or higher

Safest choice: 16 GB RAM or higher

Some Linux distributions (known as distros) will work fine with just 2 GB RAM.

Processor or CPU (Central Processing Unit)

Think of it as the device's brain.

Minimum: 2.5 GHz

Recommended: 2.5 GHz or higher

Safest choice: 3.0 GHz or higher

Some Linux distributions (known as distros) will work fine with weaker CPUs.

Graphics Card or GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)

The GPU is very useful for 3D rendering. Video games and video editing is made easier with this hardware.

Minimum: Any GPU will do for most tasks.

Recommended: RTX 2000s series, RX 6000s series or greater

Safest choice: Latest GPU available

Recommended: Full HD (1080p) + 60hz

Safest choice: 4K or higher + 60hz or higher

For laptops, the greater the resolution and refresh rate, the more battery the screen will consume.

Storage

Recommended: 512 GB or higher

Safest choice: 1024 GB (1 TB) or higher

Camera

Recommended: 12 MP, 1080p @ 30FPS or greater

Safest choice: +12MP, 4K @ +30FPS or greater

Recommended: 30Wh or greater

MacOS (iMac or MacBook)

iMacs:

Minimum: iMac 2019 series

Safest choice: iMac 2023 series or newer

MacBooks:

Minimum: MacBook 2018 series

Safest choice: MacBook 2023 series or newer

For operating systems, make sure you also have access to security updates. OLED/QLED/AMOLED screens usually have better image quality, but they are prone to burn-in. Megapixels don't always define camera quality.
